It can be done.

Tuition in many parts of Europe is free, or almost free.

That's because their social culture embraces all levels of education as a right — not a privilege.

Germany

All German states have abolished tuition for undergraduate students.

Norway

Public universities do not charge tuition fees, only a student union fee of around 50 Euro per semester.

Finland

Free tuition for EU students. Foreign students may be charged if the study program is conducted in a language other than Finnish or Swedish.

Sweden

Free tuition for EU students, exchange-program students, and Ph.D students, but you pay a small registration fee.

Slovenia

EU students don't pay tuition, and neither do nationals of countries with reciprocal agreements.
